What Is Dimensional Weight (Volumetric Weight)?

Dimensional weight, also called volumetric weight, is a pricing method used by carriers such as DHL, UPS and FedEx. Instead of charging only by actual weight, shipping companies calculate the space a package occupies. The chargeable weight is the greater of actual weight or dimensional weight.

Using a dimensional weight calculator ensures that you correctly estimate shipping costs before sending your goods. This is essential for Amazon FBA sellers, Shopify store owners and international freight shipments.

How to Calculate Dimensional Weight

The formula typically used by carriers is:

(Length × Width × Height) ÷ Dimensional Factor

Common dimensional factors:

  • Air freight: 6000 (cm) or 166 (inches)
  • Express carriers: 5000 to 6000
  • Sea freight: based on CBM (cubic meter)
