What Is Dimensional Weight?

Dimensional weight (also called volumetric weight) is a pricing method used by carriers. Instead of charging only by actual weight, companies like FedEx, DHL, UPS and Amazon FBA calculate shipping cost based on package volume.

Formula in centimeters:
Length × Width × Height ÷ DIM factor

DIM Factors by Carrier (2026)

  • FedEx: 5000 (international) / 139 in inches
  • DHL: 5000
  • UPS: 6000 (some services 5000)
  • Amazon FBA: 5000 standard

Example Calculation

FAQ

How do I calculate volumetric weight in cm?

Multiply length × width × height in centimeters and divide by the carrier’s DIM factor (5000 or 6000).

Why is my shipping cost higher than expected?

If your package is large but light, carriers will charge using dimensional weight instead of actual weight.

What is the difference between actual and dimensional weight?

The carrier charges whichever is greater between actual scale weight and calculated volumetric weight.
